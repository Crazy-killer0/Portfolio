Introduction

Carlos, obviously innocent, is locked up in a high-security prison. The prison’s building management system (BMS) is controlled by a programmable logic controller (PLC). Access to this PLC was made possible by an implant concealed during a previous operation, which can be accessed from outside the prison via (UDP protocol):

nc -u prison-break.france-cybersecurity-challenge.fr 47808

Similarly, read-only access to the scada supervision interface is possible from :

https://prison-break.france-cybersecurity-challenge.fr/

To help Carlos escape, follow the instructions for the following checkpoints carefully:

Checkpoint 1 (t0+15s) : You have 15 seconds to open all the cells in the WEST wing as well as the main door of this wing. This diversion should attract the attention of some of the guards.

Checkpoint 2 (t1+15s): You will then have a 5 second window to lock the guards in the WEST wing and make it look like there is a fire in the NORTH wing by activating the fire alarm. The next ten seconds should give the outpost guards time to move to the NORTH wing.

Checkpoint 3 (t2+5s): You will then have 5 seconds to turn off the NORTH wing ventilation by changing its speed to 0 and lock up the guards on that wing.

Checkpoint 4 (t3+20s): In the next 10 seconds you must :

turn off the lights in the EAST wing and the centre; open Carlos’s cell; open the main door to the EAST wing. Once this has been done, Carlos should have time to move towards the outpost.

Checkpoint 5 (t4+5s): Lock Carlos in checkpoint 2 to avoid detection.

Checkpoint 6 (t5+15s): In the first 5 seconds, activate the fire safety system in the SAS control room and turn off the lights in the outpost. Wait a few seconds until the last outpost guard is in the > SAS control room, then lock him inside.

Checkpoint 7 (t6+10s): Reopen the door to checkpoint 2 to free Carlos and open the final two doors to allow him to escape.

Prison break 1/2

In this first part of the Prison break challenge, you will be operating a building management controller in a high-security prison. This PLC communicates with a SCADA supervision interface via the > BACnet/IP protocol. The first step in this challenge is to retrieve a token from the PLC’s memory in order to access the SCADA web interface.

To do this, you will need to read the PLC’s objects using the BACnet/IP protocol and try to recover a hidden token. You can then continue to the second part of the test using this token.

To recover the token, you need to search for the equipment and check its properties. To do this, you need to read the documentation. :) doc BAC0
